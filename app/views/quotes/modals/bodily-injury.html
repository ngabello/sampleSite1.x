What Is Bodily Injury Liability?

In an accident where you / driver of your car are at fault, Bodily Injury will cover expenses for medical costs, lost wages, sickness, death related to the accident. This coverage also pays for pain and suffering that may result from the accident. It is part of the liability coverage portion of your policy.

Your Bodily Injury coverage is displayed in the form of two numbers. The first dollar amount represents the coverage limit for any one person and the second dollar amount represents the total coverage limit for on accident. For example, you have $50,000 / $100,000 Bodily Injury coverage. You will have up to $50,000 in coverage for one person's injuries and up to $100,000 in total coverage for the accident. Your coverage limits, deductibles, and certain exclusions may apply. Please read your Elephant Insurance Company policy for details.

How Much Coverage Do I Need?

Individual states have different minimum requirements for liability insurance and for lease cars. In Texas the minimum is $30,000/$60,000. If you lease a car, your lease contract will typically require you to maintain minimum Bodily Injury Liability coverage of $100,000/$300,000. You should buy enough Bodily Injury Liability coverage to fully protect your assets.
